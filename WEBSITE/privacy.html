Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 17, 2026 · Last updated: April 17, 2026

ChipFindr ("we," "us," or "our") operates the ChipFindr mobile application (iOS and Android) and the website at chipfindr.netlify.app (collectively, the "Service"). This policy describes how we collect, use, store, and share information when you use our Service.

By using ChipFindr, you agree to the collection and use of information as described in this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

Quick summary

We collect only what we need to run the app. We never sell your data. We never share your personal info with advertisers. The only reason we store owner contact info is so lost pets can be reunited with their families.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Account information

When you create an account, we collect your email address and, optionally, your name. If you sign in with Apple or Google, we receive only the information you authorize those services to share (typically your email address and display name). We do not receive or store your Apple ID password or Google password.

1.2 Microchip lookup data

When you search for a microchip number, we log the chip number, whether a match was found, the source registry, a timestamp, and a device identifier. This data powers your lookup history and helps us improve the Service.

1.3 ChipFindr Registry data

When you register a pet's microchip through the app or website, we collect and store the information you provide: chip number, pet name, species, breed, owner name, phone number, city/state, and email address. This information is stored in our database so that veterinarians, shelters, and animal control officers searching for that chip number can locate the pet's owner.

1.4 Bulk import data

Veterinary clinics and shelters may upload CSV files containing multiple chip registrations. This data is treated identically to individual registrations and is subject to the same policies.

1.5 Device and usage data

We collect standard analytics data including device type, operating system version, app version, screen views, feature usage, and crash reports. We use Mixpanel for analytics. This data is used to improve the app's performance and user experience.

1.6 Bluetooth data

When you pair a Bluetooth RFID chip reader, we process the chip number transmitted by the reader. We log basic connection events (reader name, connection time, disconnection time) for diagnostic purposes to improve scanner compatibility. We do not collect scanner serial numbers, firmware data, or other hardware identifiers beyond the reader's Bluetooth display name. We do not share Bluetooth connection data with third parties, including hardware manufacturers.

1.7 Location data

During onboarding, ChipFindr may request access to your device's location services. If granted, we use your approximate location solely to provide context-relevant results (such as identifying nearby shelters or clinics). We do not continuously track your location in the background. We do not sell or share location data with third parties. If you provide a city/state when registering a chip, that is user-entered data. You can revoke location access at any time in your device settings.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • Reunite lost pets. Owner contact information in the ChipFindr Registry is displayed to veterinarians, shelters, and animal control officers who search for a matching chip number. This is the core purpose of the Service.
  • Provide and improve the Service. We use lookup logs and analytics to monitor app performance, fix bugs, and develop new features.
  • Communicate with you. We may send transactional emails related to your account (e.g., password resets). We do not send marketing emails unless you opt in.
  • Prevent misuse. We use device identifiers and usage patterns to detect and prevent bulk scraping, spam registrations, and other abuse.

3. How We Share Your Information

3.1 Registry data is intentionally public

The owner contact information you submit to the ChipFindr Registry (name, phone, city) is accessible to anyone who searches for that specific chip number. This is by design — the purpose of a pet microchip registry is to make this information findable when a lost pet is scanned. Email addresses are stored but not displayed in search results.

3.2 Third-party services

We use the following third-party services:

  • Firebase (Google) — authentication, database, and hosting. Firebase Privacy Policy
  • Mixpanel — analytics. Mixpanel Privacy Policy
  • Apple (Sign in with Apple) — authentication. Apple Privacy Policy
  • Google (Sign in with Google) — authentication. Google Privacy Policy

3.3 We do not sell your data

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for advertising or marketing purposes. We do not display ads in the app.

3.4 Legal requirements

We may disclose your information if required by law, subpoena, court order, or government request, or if we believe disclosure is necessary to protect our rights, your safety, or the safety of others.

4. The ChipFindr Registry

ChipFindr operates its own pet microchip registry. When you register a chip, the data you provide is stored in our database hosted on Google Firebase (Firestore). Key points:

  • Your registration is free and permanent — there are no renewal fees.
  • You can update or delete your registration at any time by contacting us or through the app.
  • We do not verify the accuracy of registration data. You are responsible for providing accurate and current information.
  • If a chip is already registered by its owner in our system, bulk imports from clinics or shelters will not overwrite the existing registration.
  • The ChipFindr Registry is one of multiple registries we search. We also query public registries including the AAHA Universal Pet Microchip Lookup network and others.

5. Third-Party Registry Searches

When a chip number is not found in our own registry, ChipFindr searches public microchip registries (such as those participating in the AAHA network) on your behalf. These searches work by loading the registry's public search page within the app — functionally identical to you opening the website in a browser and typing the chip number yourself. Important distinctions:

  • We display results from third-party registries to you in real time. We do not copy, store, or add this data to the ChipFindr Registry.
  • Results may be cached locally on your device for offline access (Pro feature only). This cache expires after 30 days and is stored in your app's sandboxed storage.
  • We do not redistribute, resell, or permanently store data obtained from third-party registries on our servers.
  • The chip number you search is transmitted to the third-party registry's website as part of the search — this is inherent to how their public search works.

6. Data Storage and Security

  • Account data and registry data are stored on Google Firebase servers in the United States.
  • Data in transit is encrypted via TLS/SSL.
  • Data at rest in Firebase is encrypted using Google's default encryption.
  • Offline cached data on your device is stored in your app's sandboxed storage and is only accessible to ChipFindr.
  • We implement Firestore security rules that validate all writes and restrict unauthorized access.

While we take reasonable measures to protect your data, no system is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Data Retention

  • Account data: Retained until you delete your account.
  • Lookup history: Retained for the life of your account. You can clear your history in the app.
  • Registry data: Retained indefinitely to ensure pets remain findable. You can request deletion at any time.
  • Offline cache: Automatically expires after 30 days on-device.
  • Analytics data: Retained per Mixpanel's data retention policies.

8. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights:

  • Access: Request a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Correction: Update or correct your personal information.
  • Deletion: Request deletion of your account and associated data.
  • Portability: Request your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Objection: Object to certain processing of your data.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at chipfindr@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days.

9. Children's Privacy

ChipFindr is not directed at children under 13 (or 16 in the EU). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, contact us and we will delete it promptly.

10. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to know what personal information we collect, request its deletion, and opt out of its sale. We do not sell personal information. To make a request, contact us at chipfindr@gmail.com.

11. International Users

If you access ChipFindr from outside the United States, your data is transferred to and stored in the United States. By using the Service, you consent to this transfer.
